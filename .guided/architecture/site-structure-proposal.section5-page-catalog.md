### Detailed Page Descriptions

#### 1. Home Page (`/`)

**Page Title**: Career Topologies  
**Primary Audience**: All audiences (first-time visitors)  
**Description**: Central hub providing welcoming entry point that explains what Career Topologies is, highlights value propositions for different audiences, and showcases three topology models visually.

**Key Content Blocks**:
- **Hero Section**: Tagline, version badge (v1.0.0), hero image showing Y/W/Network topologies, primary CTA "Explore the Framework"
- **Audience Section**: Four audience cards (Technology Leaders, HR/People Teams, Engineering Managers, Individual Contributors) with icons and descriptions
- **Framework Overview**: Four component cards linking to Topologies, Leveling, Progression Pillars, and Shapes
- **Value Proposition**: Open-source positioning, research-backed claims, transparency benefits
- **Call-to-Action Footer**: Multiple CTAs to Framework, Manifesto, and Contributing

**Interactive Elements**: Navigation cards, CTA buttons  
**Visual Components**: Hero topology illustration, audience icons, component cards

#### 3. Manifesto Page (`/manifesto`)

**Page Title**: Career Manifesto  
**Primary Audience**: Values-driven stakeholders, technology leaders, change agents  
**Description**: Establishes philosophical foundation through eight guiding principles that inform all framework design choices and serve as values alignment tool for organizations.

**Key Content Blocks**:
- **Introduction**: Why principles matter for career development
- **Eight Principles** (numbered icon cards):
  1. **Transparency** - Clear, documented, accessible expectations
  2. **Fair Recognition** - Impact-based advancement, not politics
  3. **Equality of Opportunity** - Multiple paths honoring different strengths
  4. **Growth Mindset** - Proficiency is developed, not innate
  5. **Evidence-Based** - Decisions grounded in demonstrated impact
  6. **Continuous Feedback** - Ongoing dialogue, not annual reviews alone
  7. **Flexibility & Experimentation** - Support for exploration and role changes
  8. **Open & Evolving** - Community-driven continuous improvement
- **Living the Manifesto**: How principles translate to practice
- **Call to Action**: Join the movement, explore framework, contribute

**Interactive Elements**: Icon cards with expandable content (possible)  
**Visual Components**: Eight principle icons, visual categorization

#### 4. Core Concepts Page (`/concepts`)

**Page Title**: Core Concepts  
**Primary Audience**: New visitors building understanding, all users needing quick reference  
**Description**: Foundational glossary defining seven core concepts with clear definitions, contextual explanations, and cross-references to detailed pages.

**Key Content Blocks**:
- **Introduction**: Purpose of shared vocabulary
- **Seven Core Concepts** (numbered sections):
  1. **Career Topology** - Structural model for career paths
  2. **Career Level** - Position based on impact and autonomy
  3. **Progression Pillar** - Competency dimension for growth
  4. **Proficiency** - Competence level on specific pillar (1-5 scale)
  5. **Impact** - Scope of organizational effect
  6. **Autonomy** - Degree of decision-making independence
  7. **Professional Shape** - Skill breadth vs. depth profile
- **How Concepts Interconnect**: Explanation of relationships
- **Applying Concepts in Practice**: Use cases for career conversations, reviews, promotions

**Interactive Elements**: Cross-reference links to detailed pages  
**Visual Components**: None (opportunity for conceptual relationship diagram)

#### 5. Topologies Page (`/topologies`)

**Page Title**: Career Topologies  
**Primary Audience**: Technology leaders, HR teams selecting organizational model  
**Description**: Comparative analysis of three career topology models (Y-shaped, W-shaped, Network) with visual diagrams, selection guidance, and suitability criteria.

**Key Content Blocks**:
- **Introduction**: What topologies are and why they matter
- **Y-Model (Dual Track)**: IC and Manager bifurcation at senior level, visual diagram
- **W-Model (Tri-Track)**: Technical Lead track with interim management experiences, visual diagram
- **Network Model**: Fluid lateral movement without rigid hierarchies, visual diagram
- **Comparative Summary Table**: Side-by-side feature comparison
- **Selection Guidance**: Which topology fits different organizational contexts, maturity stages, cultures
- **Related Pages**: Links to Framework, Leveling, Management

**Interactive Elements**: Model comparison interactions (possible)  
**Visual Components**: Y-Model diagram, W-Model diagram, Network Model diagram, comparative table

#### 10. Shapes Page (`/shapes`)

**Page Title**: Skill Profiles (Shapes)  
**Primary Audience**: Individual contributors, managers planning development  
**Description**: Professional skill profile types (I-shaped, T-shaped, Pi-shaped) explaining breadth vs. depth strategies and growth transitions.

**Key Content Blocks**:
- **Introduction**: What skill profiles are and why they matter
- **Three Professional Shapes**:
  1. **I-Shaped (Deep Specialist)**: Single domain expertise, vertical depth
  2. **T-Shaped (Specialist + Breadth)**: Deep expertise plus cross-disciplinary awareness
  3. **Pi-Shaped (Multi-Domain Expert)**: Two deep domains with broad foundation
- **Growth and Transitions**: How professionals evolve from I → T → Pi
- **Relationship to Levels**: How shapes complement leveling framework
- **Development Strategies**: Intentional breadth-building, second domain cultivation
- **Related Pages**: Links to Progression, Leveling, Topologies

**Interactive Elements**: Related pages navigation  
**Visual Components**: None (opportunity for shape diagrams)

**Primary Audience**: Managers, HR teams, aspiring leaders

**Key Content Blocks**:
- **Why It Matters**: Importance of intentional leadership development
- **Leadership Readiness Flowchart** (4 steps, 3 outcomes):
  - **Step 1**: Assess interest and motivation
  - **Step 2**: Evaluate core leadership competencies
  - **Step 3**: Provide interim management experiences (tech lead, project lead)
  - **Step 4**: Make promotion decision
  - **Outcomes**: Promote to Manager, Continue Development, Return to IC Track
- **Best Practices**: Structured assessment, interim experiences, mentor support
- **Avoiding Pitfalls**: Preventing "accidental managers" promoted solely for technical excellence
- **Related Pages**: Links to Management, Progression, Framework

**Interactive Elements**: Interactive flowchart with hover tooltips (desktop) or tap details (mobile)  
**Visual Components**: Leadership Readiness Flowchart (4-step decision process with conditional branches)
